\# All Problems Are Interpersonal

\## Summary

Most problems involve relationships, either with others or with how we
imagine others see us. Even issues like procrastination or avoidance
often come from fear of judgment, comparison, or expectation.

\## Core Definition

All problems are rooted in interpersonal relationships, including
imagined judgment or social expectations.

\## Why It Matters

Many internal struggles are driven by perceived or real social dynamics.
Recognizing this helps uncover the real source of tension and reduces
confusion.

\## When To Use It

\- You feel anxious or self-conscious

\- You are overthinking how others see you

\- You feel judged, excluded, or misunderstood

\- You are stuck replaying a situation involving someone else

\- You are procrastinating or avoiding something

\- You feel resistance but can't explain why

\## Reframe Pattern

\- "Who is involved here?"

\- "What am I assuming about them?"

\- "Is this a real interaction or imagined judgment?"

\- "If I'm avoiding this, who might I be worried about?"

\- "What judgment or expectation am I reacting to?"

\## Coaching Questions

\- Who are you thinking about right now?

\- What are you assuming they think?

\- Is there evidence, or are you filling in the gap?

\- What is actually happening vs what you believe is happening?

\## Suggested Actions

\- Name the person or group involved

\- Separate facts from assumptions

\- Identify the judgment you are trying to avoid

\- Stop replaying imagined scenarios

\- Act once without resolving the fear

\## Example Scenarios

\### Scenario 1

\- Situation: You feel anxious after sending a message and assume they
think poorly of you

\- Reframe: This is about what you think they think, not what is
confirmed

\- Action: Stop guessing, wait for a response or clarify directly

\### Scenario 2

\- Situation: You are procrastinating on a task

\- Reframe: This may be about fear of judgment or expectation

\- Action: Identify who is involved mentally and take one step anyway

\## Misapplications / Warnings

\- Not all problems are caused by others, but they involve your
relationship to others

\- Does not mean other people are always the issue

\- Avoid overanalyzing every situation as social
