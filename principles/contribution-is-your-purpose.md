\# Contribution Is Your Purpose

\## Summary

A sense of meaning and happiness comes from contributing to others, not
from seeking approval or focusing only on yourself. When you shift
toward contribution, many internal struggles lose their weight.

\## Core Definition

Your role is to contribute to others in a meaningful way, rather than
seeking validation or focusing only on yourself.

\## Why It Matters

Focusing only on yourself often leads to emptiness or overthinking.
Contribution creates purpose, direction, and fulfillment.

\## When To Use It

\- You feel stuck, lost, or lacking direction

\- You are focused on how you are perceived

\- You feel unmotivated or disconnected

\- You are overthinking your own situation

\- You are seeking validation or recognition

\## Reframe Pattern

\- "How can I contribute here?"

\- "Who can I help right now?"

\- "If I want meaning, I need to contribute"

\- "Happiness comes from contribution, not attention"

\## Coaching Questions

\- Who could benefit from your effort right now?

\- What value can you provide in this situation?

\- Are you focused on being seen, or being useful?

\- What is one small way you can contribute immediately?

\## Suggested Actions

\- Take one action that is useful to someone else, even if small

\- Shift attention from yourself to others or the situation

\- Offer value without expecting recognition

\- Do something useful, even if it feels small

\## Example Scenarios

\### Scenario 1

\- Situation: You feel unmotivated and unsure what to do

\- Reframe: Instead of focusing on yourself, look for where you can
contribute

\- Action: Help someone or move something forward

\### Scenario 2

\- Situation: You are seeking approval after completing something

\- Reframe: The value is in the contribution, not the response

\- Action: Move on to the next useful action

\## Misapplications / Warnings

\- Not about ignoring your own needs completely

\- Contribution should not turn into people-pleasing

\- Focus on meaningful contribution, not overextending
