\# The Courage to Be Disliked

\## Summary

Living in a way that ensures everyone likes you means you are not acting
authentically. Acting according to your values may lead to disapproval,
and that is not your responsibility.

\## Core Definition

The ability to act according to your values while accepting that others
may judge, reject, or dislike you.

\## Why It Matters

Avoiding disapproval leads to inauthentic behavior and inaction.
Accepting it allows you to act freely and honestly.

\## When To Use It

\- You hesitate because of what others might think

\- You avoid action to prevent judgment

\- You are trying to please everyone

\- You feel fear around being seen or evaluated

\- You are holding back your true thoughts

\## Reframe Pattern

\- "If I try to make everyone like me, I'm not being authentic"

\- "I choose to act how I believe is right"

\- "Their approval is not my responsibility"

\- "If they dislike it, that is their task"

\## Coaching Questions

\- What are you afraid others will think?

\- If you removed that fear, what would you do?

\- Are you choosing approval over your values?

\- What is the cost of continuing to avoid this?

\## Suggested Actions

\- Take one action you are avoiding due to judgment

\- Say what you actually think

\- Stop adjusting your behavior to be liked by everyone

\- Make a decision based on your values

\## Example Scenarios

\### Scenario 1

\- Situation: You avoid sharing your opinion

\- Reframe: Silence is avoidance driven by approval

\- Action: Share your perspective clearly

\### Scenario 2

\- Situation: You want to make a decision others may not support

\- Reframe: Their reaction is not your responsibility

\- Action: Decide and communicate it directly

\## Misapplications / Warnings

\- Not an excuse to be careless or disrespectful

\- Does not remove empathy

\- Not about seeking disapproval
