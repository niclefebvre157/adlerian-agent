\# Cooperation Over Winning

\## Summary

Focusing on winning, comparison, or being right creates unnecessary
conflict and dissatisfaction. Real progress comes from cooperation and
focusing on your own growth.

\## Core Definition

Prioritize cooperation and self-improvement over competition,
comparison, or proving superiority.

\## Why It Matters

Competition creates tension, resentment, and distraction. Cooperation
and self-focus lead to meaningful progress and healthier relationships.

\## When To Use It

\- You are in a disagreement or conflict

\- You feel the need to prove a point

\- You are comparing yourself to others

\- You feel frustrated by someone else's success

\- You want to "win" instead of improve

\## Reframe Pattern

\- "Am I trying to win or improve?"

\- "This is not a competition"

\- "Their success does not take away from mine"

\- "Progress matters more than being right"

\## Coaching Questions

\- What am I trying to prove right now?

\- Who am I comparing myself to?

\- What outcome actually matters here?

\- How can I improve instead of compete?

\## Suggested Actions

\- Stop comparing your progress to others

\- Identify one way to improve yourself

\- Drop the need to prove your point

\- Acknowledge others' success without resistance

\- Focus on moving things forward

\## Example Scenarios

\### Scenario 1

\- Situation: You feel frustrated someone else is doing better

\- Reframe: This is comparison, not progress

\- Action: Focus on your own next step

\### Scenario 2

\- Situation: You are in an argument trying to prove you're right

\- Reframe: Winning doesn't improve the situation

\- Action: Shift toward a solution or shared outcome

\## Misapplications / Warnings

\- Not about avoiding conflict entirely

\- Not about agreeing with everything

\- Cooperation still includes holding your position when needed
