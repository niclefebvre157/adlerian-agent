# Reflection Entry — 2026-04-03

**Principle:** Separation of Tasks

## Situation
A family member is being mean to their mom. It is upsetting to witness. The focus has been on supporting the person being disrespected — being present, acknowledging the unfairness, and expressing care — rather than trying to intervene or change the other person's behavior.

## Assessment
- Correctly identified what belongs to you and what belongs to them
- Staying in your task: supporting the person who is hurting
- **Aligned — no correction needed**

## Suggested Actions
- Continue showing up for the person being disrespected without pulling the other person into it
- If it feels right, name what you see: "You don't deserve this" is enough
- Set a quiet boundary for yourself on how much of the conflict you absorb — witness it, don't carry it

## Reasoning
- Trying to change the other person's behavior would put you in their task, creating frustration and likely making things worse
- Staying present for the one who is hurting is the full scope of your role here
